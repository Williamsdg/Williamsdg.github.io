Since 1928

A Birmingham Institution

The doors of the Irondale Cafe first opened in 1928 as Emmett Montgomery's hot dog stand. Maggie Prentice added hamburgers and sandwiches, and in 1932, Bess Fortenberry purchased the business and transformed it into the beloved Southern cafeteria it is today.

Nearly a century later, the tradition continues — everything cooked from scratch daily, served cafeteria-style on real plates, just the way Bess would have wanted it.

As Seen On Screen

The Cafe That Inspired Fried Green Tomatoes

Bess Fortenberry's niece, author Fannie Flagg, grew up eating at this very cafeteria line. She immortalized it in her 1987 bestselling novel Fried Green Tomatoes at the Whistle Stop Cafe, which became the beloved 1991 film starring Kathy Bates and Jessica Tandy.

"The Whistle Stop Cafe in the book is the Irondale Cafe. I didn't have to make up a thing."

— Fannie Flagg

Since the movie premiered in Birmingham, visitors from all 50 states and over 30 countries have made the pilgrimage to taste the original fried green tomatoes — 600 to 800 slices served fresh every single day.
